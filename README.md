## Program design

### Learning objective

The school was intended to help members understand terminology, establish a repeatable decision process, and discuss risk before attempting to interpret live-market content. It did not guarantee competency, profitability, or suitability for options trading.

### Curriculum architecture

- **Foundations:** calls, puts, strike price, expiration, premium, and worked examples.
- **Account and risk concepts:** position sizing, discipline, process over short-term outcomes, exits, and responding to losses.
- **Applied setups:** annotated charts and examples drawn from historical sessions.
- **Reinforcement:** live explanation, recurring reviews, member questions, and community discussion.

### Delivery and operations

The written curriculum sat inside a broader operating cadence of premarket discussion, live commentary, time-stamped alerts, after-hours recap documents, edited videos, Sunday discussions, and weekly chart reviews. Lessons were designed for members using small-account examples as well as larger accounts because both groups were represented in the community.

## What is included

- `lessons/01-options-basics.md` - 'What are options in trading?' from January 2022, covering calls, puts, strike, expiration, premium, and a worked long-call example.
- `lessons/02-account-risk-management.md` - 'Account management' from July 2022, covering historical position-sizing examples, process discipline, exits, and loss-management discussion.

Both lessons were converted from the original member-facing documents with light formatting cleanup. The original instructional voice has been retained as an artifact; it should not be interpreted as updated guidance.

## Editorial and safety context

Historical wording requires current qualification:

- A purchased option can generally expire worthless and the buyer's loss is generally limited to premium and transaction costs; that statement does not apply to every options position. Written or multi-leg options can create materially different and potentially greater risk.
- Numerical account-sizing and stop examples in the archived lessons were historical teaching examples, not individualized recommendations.
- Terms such as 'successful trader' describe the original curriculum voice and do not imply an evidenced outcome.
- Options terminology, platform capabilities, market structure, and regulatory requirements may have changed since 2022.

Each directly published lesson should carry the same archival notice and an editor's-note section where historical statements need qualification. Options involve substantial risk and are not appropriate for every person.

## What is not included

The 48-page setups workbook, 'Setups, Jul 2022-2023,' is not public because its pages embed Discord screenshots containing member usernames and avatars. Member privacy takes priority over portfolio completeness. The two published lessons are representative of the curriculum's teaching style but not the entire program.
