# MAK Trading School

The education arm of Trade with MAK, a paid day-trading community I ran from 2021 to 2023 (see the `mak-trading-case-study` repo for the full business story).

## What it was

MAK Trading School was the structured learning track inside the community. While the trading floor covered live markets in real time, the school gave newer members a foundation: what options are, how to size positions and manage an account, and a library of annotated trade setups drawn from live sessions. Lessons were written for members at small-account scale ($2k–$5k) as well as larger accounts, because that was who actually joined.

## What is included

- `lessons/01-options-basics.md` — "What are options in trading?" (January 2022). Calls, puts, strike, expiration, premium, and a worked example of a call trade.
- `lessons/02-account-risk-management.md` — "Account management" (July 2022). Position sizing, small vs. large account sizing rules, process over profits, securing profits, and dealing with losses.

Both lessons are converted from the original member-facing documents with only light formatting cleanup; the substance is unchanged.

## What is not included, and why

The school also had a 48-page setups workbook ("Setups, Jul 2022–2023") compiling annotated chart setups from live sessions. It is not published here: the pages embed Discord chat screenshots that show member usernames and avatars, and member privacy takes priority over portfolio completeness. The written lessons above are representative of the curriculum's teaching style.

## Honest context

This material was written for the 2022 market — a high-volatility, news-driven bear market in US equities and index options. The setups, examples, and risk numbers reflect those conditions and that era's tooling (ThinkOrSwim, Discord). It is preserved here as a record of the curriculum I built and taught, not as current trading advice.

None of this is financial advice. Options trading involves substantial risk of loss.
